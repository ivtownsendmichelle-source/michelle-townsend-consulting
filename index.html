My Story

I've seen the burnout
firsthand.

I spent over eight years in nonprofit and community organizing — building the Cave Junction Farmers Market from the ground up, watching dedicated people pour everything they had into work that mattered. I saw brilliant, passionate organizers drowning in repetitive tasks, grant reports, email chains, and administrative work that had nothing to do with why they showed up.

As Co-President of the Oregon Farmers Markets Association — a statewide organization with 136 member markets — I've met hundreds of these organizations. They have zero tech teams, minimal budgets, and enormous hearts. They deserve the same competitive advantages that large corporations take for granted.

💡 That's why I got into AI consulting. Not because it's trendy — because I believe the right tools, introduced the right way, can give small organizations their time back.

How I Work

My approach is simple.
On purpose.

I've seen too many "solutions" that confuse the people they're supposed to help. I do it differently.

01
I meet you where you are.
No jargon, no judgment, no assumptions about what you already know. We start from your reality, not a tech demo. If you've never touched an AI tool, that's completely fine — that's exactly where we begin.
02
I build with you, not for you.
You'll understand every tool we create together. When I leave, you're not dependent on me — you own it. My goal is to make myself unnecessary as fast as possible.
03
Small changes, big impact.
We start with the easiest wins first. The goal isn't to transform your entire organization overnight — it's to get you a meaningful win in the first session, then build from there.
What a first engagement looks like
  • We talk about what's eating your team's time right now
  • I identify 2–3 places AI could help immediately
  • We pick the easiest one and implement it together
  • Your team actually uses it (and knows why it works)
  • We document everything so you own it fully
  • You decide if you want to keep going
