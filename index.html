My name is Michelle Townsend. AI is advancing faster than anyone was ready for.

When the light bulb was invented, years passed between the technology being available and people actually adopting it. But now adoption is speeding up like a rubber band slingshotting us into the future without time to put checks and balances into place.

I realized that all the small organizations I love, the nonprofits, the food banks, the community groups, might not be able to see the potential pitfalls before it's too late.

Did you know there are popular note-taking tools people are bringing into Zoom meetings that can mine your data, scrub your websites, take your financial information somewhere you don't know about, and train new models with your sensitive information?

Organizational health means dealing with this now. Not later.

My Story

I'm not a tech person who decided to help nonprofits. I'm a community organizer who's been doing this work for over eight years. That hasn't changed.

What changed is I started paying attention to AI and I saw a giant gap. Nobody in the nonprofit world was talking about policy. Nobody was thinking about data protection. Nobody was asking the hard questions about what these tools are actually doing with our information.

And the people who ARE talking about AI? They're tech bros who've never set foot in a food bank. They don't understand this world. I do. I live in it.

So I'm not shifting careers. I'm expanding the work I've always done. Because these organizations deserve someone in their corner who understands both the tools and the mission. And the sooner they have that, the more choices they get to make for themselves instead of having those choices made for them.

What I Do

The first thing I do is look at your organizational hygiene. Are your documents living in one place or scattered across five people's desktops? Does everyone who needs access have it? Are your passwords secure or is someone's login on a sticky note? Who holds all the institutional knowledge and what happens if they leave tomorrow? We get that cleaned up first because nothing else works if the foundation is a mess.

From there we go deeper. What tools are you using and are they the right ones? What happens to your data when someone on your team downloads a free AI note-taker into a Zoom meeting? Do you have any policies around that? Most small orgs don't. And that's not a failure. It's just that nobody's had the time to think about it. That's what I'm here for.

I also look at the bigger picture. Is your board up to date on what AI means for your organization? Do your bylaws account for data governance? Is your mission still driving your decisions or has it drifted? These are the kinds of questions that protect an organization's future. I can help you draft policy recommendations, prepare board presentations, and build the kind of strategic framework that keeps you focused on your mission instead of getting pulled off course.

And when you need things built, I build them. A website. A custom tool that fits your actual workflow. A system that takes something your team does manually every week and makes it take five minutes instead of five hours. Whatever the organization actually needs.
