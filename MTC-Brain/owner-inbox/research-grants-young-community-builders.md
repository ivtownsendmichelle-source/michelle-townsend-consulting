# Research: Grants for Young High School Graduates Starting Community Organizations in Southern Oregon

**Researcher:** Dexter, Senior Researcher
**Reviewed by:** Maxwell, Head Agent
**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Requested by:** Michelle

## Summary

Researched grant and funding opportunities for young high school graduates in Southern Oregon who want to skip college and instead start a nonprofit or meaningful community project. Found a mix of Oregon-specific foundation grants, federal service programs, and national fellowships. Most grant funding flows to organizations, not individuals. The clearest paths for a young person are service programs (AmeriCorps), fiscal sponsorship under an existing nonprofit, or applying through national fellowships designed for young founders.

---

## Oregon-Specific Opportunities

### Oregon Community Foundation (OCF) - Community Grants
Flexible funding for organizations addressing pressing community needs. The Spring 2026 cycle explicitly prioritizes small rural organizations. OCF also recently awarded $545,000 to Southern Oregon nonprofits. A young person would need to form or partner with a 501(c)(3) to access this.

- [OCF Community Grants Program](https://oregoncf.org/grants-and-scholarships/grants/community-grant-program)
- [2026 Spring Grants Cycle FAQ](https://oregoncf.org/grants-and-scholarships/grants/community-grant-program/community-grant-program-faqs)

### OCF - Thriving Entrepreneurs Grant Program
Funds nonprofits that support entrepreneurs. Prioritizes organizations serving women, people of color, and people in under-resourced rural communities. Not a direct grant to an individual, but a young person working with a nonprofit incubator could benefit.

- [OCF Thriving Entrepreneurs](https://oregoncf.org/grants-and-scholarships/grants/thriving-entrepreneurs-grant-program)

### The Ford Family Foundation
Based in Roseburg. Makes grants to public charities and agencies in rural Oregon communities under 35,000 population. Funding areas include youth development, education, community capacity, and economic vitality. Illinois Valley qualifies geographically. Applicants must be 501(c)(3) organizations, government entities, or recognized sovereign entities.

- [Ford Family Foundation](https://www.tfff.org/)
- [Ford Family Foundation Rural Youth Development Grant (Education Northwest)](https://educationnorthwest.org/case-briefs/ford-family-foundation-rural-youth-development-grant)

### The Reser Family Foundation - Small Community Initiative
Funds economic and community development groups in rural Oregon. Once a community is approved, the foundation provides up to four annual grants of up to $15,000 each.

- [Reser Family Foundation Small Community Initiative](https://thereserfamilyfoundation.org/small-community-initiative/)

### Oregon Youth Development Division - 2025-27 Youth Grants
State-funded grants for community and school-based youth development programs serving ages 6 to 24. Focus on educational success, career development, and positive youth development. Grants go to organizations, not individuals.

- [Oregon Youth Development 2025-27 Grants](https://www.oregon.gov/youthdevelopmentdivision/youthgrants/pages/default.aspx)

## Federal Service Programs (Alternative to College)

### AmeriCorps VISTA
Full-time, one-year service placements at nonprofits fighting poverty. No college degree required. Age 18+. Members build organizational capacity: writing grants, recruiting volunteers, managing projects. Includes a living allowance, health benefits, and a choice of education award ($7,395) or end-of-service stipend ($1,800). This is the most accessible on-ramp for a young person who wants to do community work without college.

- [AmeriCorps VISTA](https://www.americorps.gov/partner/how-it-works/americorps-vista)

### USDA Rural Community Development Initiative (RCDI)
Grants to nonprofits and low-income rural communities for housing, community facilities, and economic development. Not for individuals, but a young person starting a community project could partner with an existing org that applies.

- [USDA RCDI Grants in Oregon](https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/community-facilities/rural-community-development-initiative-grants-1)

## National Fellowships and Awards

### Echoing Green Fellowship
$100,000 for early-stage social entrepreneurs building organizations that address systemic inequality. Applications for 2026 are open. Competitive. Designed for founders, not just participants. A strong fit for someone with a clear idea and a plan, but it is a highly competitive national program.

- [Echoing Green Fellowship](https://echoinggreen.org/)

### Thiel Fellowship
$200,000 over two years for people 22 or younger who want to build something instead of going to college. Equity-free. Historically focused on tech and science, but the framing is broad: "ideas that cannot wait." Very competitive. Worth knowing about even if it is a long shot.

- [Thiel Fellowship](https://thielfellowship.org/)

### Ashoka Young Changemakers
Supports high school students (grades 9 to 12) leading social change projects. The 2026 deadline was March 1, so this cycle may be closed. Worth tracking for future cohorts.

- [Ashoka Young Changemakers](https://www.ashoka.org/en-us/program/ashoka-young-changemakers)

## Flag: Something Michelle Should Know

Almost none of these grants go directly to individuals. The nonprofit sector's funding infrastructure assumes applicants are 501(c)(3) organizations. A young high school grad with a community idea has three realistic paths:

1. **Fiscal sponsorship.** Partner with an existing nonprofit that sponsors the project and receives grant funds on their behalf. This is the fastest path and does not require forming a new org.
2. **Form a 501(c)(3).** Takes time and paperwork but opens every door listed above. Oregon's filing is relatively simple. Michelle could help with this.
3. **Service first.** Do a year of AmeriCorps VISTA to build skills, network, and credibility. Then launch the project with real experience and connections.

If Michelle is being asked this question by a specific young person or family, the most practical first step is connecting them with a fiscal sponsor in the Illinois Valley or Josephine County area. The Illinois Valley 2010 Community Response Team or Josephine County's community organizations may be able to serve that role.

## What I Could Not Find

- **Grants specifically for young people who choose not to attend college.** This is not a category most funders recognize. Funding is structured around either education (scholarships) or organizations (grants). The gap between "I graduated high school" and "I have a nonprofit" is real and largely unfunded at the individual level.
- **Current fiscal sponsors active in the Illinois Valley.** I did not find a list of organizations in the area that offer fiscal sponsorship. This would need a direct outreach check.
- **Whether Oregon has a youth social enterprise incubator.** Some states have incubators specifically for young founders of social enterprises. I did not find one in Oregon, though Portland-based organizations may run something.
