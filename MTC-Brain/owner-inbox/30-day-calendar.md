This equipment is not in the way. It is the infrastructure of daily life. A good cleaner treats it with the same respect as the furniture.

### LinkedIn
**Caption:**
Kitchen organization for seniors is not about aesthetics. It is about function and safety.

Three patterns we see in almost every home:

Heavy items stored high. Cast iron pans on the top shelf, stock pots overhead. These are fall risks and strain injuries waiting to happen. Move them down or move them out if they are not being used.

Expired food in the back of the pantry. Not because anyone is careless. Because when you can not bend down or reach back easily, the front row is the only row. We pull everything out, check dates, and reorganize so nothing hides.

Cleaning supplies under the sink. Standard placement, but if someone is in a wheelchair or has trouble bending, those supplies need to move. A small caddy on the counter or a low shelf in the pantry works better.

None of this is complicated. It just takes someone thinking about the kitchen from the perspective of the person using it.
