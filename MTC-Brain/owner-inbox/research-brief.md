# Research Brief: House Cleaning for Elderly + Disabled Clients
## Cave Junction and Grants Pass, Oregon

**Prepared:** April 10, 2026
**For:** Room to Breathe (working name)

---

## 1. Existing Competitors

### Cave Junction / Illinois Valley

The options are thin. There is no established cleaning company in Cave Junction that specializes in elderly or disabled clients.

- **Redwood Cleaning Service** (Facebook-based, Cave Junction). General residential cleaning. No mention of senior specialization. Limited online presence.
- **Homeaglow** lists 49 independent cleaners in the Cave Junction area. Average rate: ~$24.50/hr. These are gig-platform cleaners, not specialized services. No screening for working with elderly or disabled clients.
- **Dano's Cleaning** and **Garcia Cleaning** appear in local reviews. Both are general residential cleaners. No senior or disability focus.
- **Care.com** lists individual housekeepers but none with stated elderly/disability specialization in Cave Junction.

Bottom line: No one in Cave Junction is marketing a cleaning service built around elderly and disabled clients. The field is wide open.

### Grants Pass

More options exist, but none own the elderly/disabled niche outright.

- **Clean Works** (cleanworksgrantspass.com). General residential and commercial cleaning. Offers a 10% senior discount. No specialized elderly/disability services described.
- **Jade Cleaning** (jadecleaning.com). Residential, commercial, and deep cleaning. Mentions "deep cleaning services for seniors" in marketing copy but does not describe a specialized program. Serves Grants Pass, Medford, Ashland, Rogue River. Hours: M-F 7am-6pm. No weekend availability. Phone: 530-572-1038.
- **Clean Freaks** (cleanfreaks.net / gpcleanfreaks.com). Maid service in Grants Pass. Home and business cleaning. No senior specialization mentioned.
- **Homeaglow** lists 30 cleaners in Grants Pass. Average rate: ~$19.96/hr.
- **Care.com** lists 28 local house cleaners in Grants Pass.

Bottom line: Some Grants Pass companies mention seniors in their copy, but no one has built their brand around it. Senior cleaning is a checkbox, not a specialty.

## 2. The Gap

No cleaning company in either market is doing all three of these things:

1. **Specializing** in elderly and disabled clients (not just offering a senior discount)
2. **Including organization** as part of the service (decluttering, setting up systems that reduce fall risk, making homes functional for limited mobility)
3. **Serving Cave Junction** at all. Most Grants Pass companies do not drive the 30 miles to Cave Junction.

A business that does all three would be the only one of its kind in the Illinois Valley and one of very few in Josephine County.

## 3. Demographics

### Josephine County (source: U.S. Census Bureau, American Community Survey)

- **Total population:** ~87,591 (2026 estimate)
- **Median age:** 47.3 years (well above national median of ~38)
- **Population 65+:** ~27% (~23,999 people). National average is 16%. This county is significantly older.
- **Households with someone 65+ living alone:** 16.1% of all households
- **Rural population:** 43.1% of county residents live in rural areas
- **Median household income:** $60,098 (below Oregon median of ~$76,000)
- **Poverty rate:** 15.6% (national average: 12.4%)
- **Largest demographic in poverty:** Women ages 55-64, followed by men 55-64

### What this means for the business

Nearly 1 in 4 residents is over 65. A significant portion live alone in rural areas. Incomes are below state average. This is a population that needs affordable, trustworthy in-home help and has fewer options than people in metro areas.

Cave Junction specifically is even more rural and more isolated than Grants Pass. Fewer services reach there. The need is higher and the supply is lower.

## 4. Funding and Referral Programs

Several Oregon programs fund or refer cleaning and housekeeping services for elderly and disabled residents. These are relevant because families looking for help often start by asking what is covered.

### Oregon Project Independence (OPI)
- **State-funded** program for people 60+ who do not have Medicaid long-term care
- Covers: personal care, housekeeping, home-delivered meals, transportation
- Client pays part of the cost (sliding scale)
- Administered locally by **Rogue Valley Council of Governments (RVCOG)**, the Area Agency on Aging for Jackson and Josephine Counties
- Contact: Aging and Disability Resource Connection, 1-855-673-2372

### OPI-M (Medicaid-funded version)
- For people with limited income/resources who qualify for Medicaid
- Services are free if eligible
- Covers: personal care, housekeeping, meal preparation, chore services, adult day services, assistive technology, home modifications
- Clients can choose how to use their allotted caregiving hours, including for housekeeping

### Chore Services Program
- Funded by the **Older Americans Act**
- Available to lower-income residents 60+
- Provides one-time deep cleaning and waste removal
- Work done by licensed professional companies
- Includes: garbage removal, deep cleaning of home interior, removal of appliances/furniture/medical equipment

### Referral pathway
A cleaning business could register with RVCOG as a qualified provider, get referrals through the AAA network, and become a known resource for case managers working with elderly and disabled clients in Josephine County.

## 7. What National Chains Miss

**MaidPro** and **Molly Maid** both market to seniors, but their actual service is standard house cleaning with senior-friendly messaging bolted on. Neither offers:

- **Organization or decluttering** as part of the service
- **Mobility-aware cleaning** (cleaning around walkers, wheelchairs, medical equipment)
- **Consistency of cleaner** (seniors need to see the same face, not a rotating crew)
- **Flexible scheduling** for medical appointments, caregiver coordination
- **Rural coverage.** Neither operates in Cave Junction. Nearest Molly Maid is in Medford. MaidPro has no Southern Oregon location.
- **Coordination with care teams** (family members, case managers, AAA referral partners)

**Maid on Call** is a niche national brand that does specialize in elderly/disabled cleaning (including meal assistance, wheelchair cleaning, mobility support). But they are not in Oregon. Their model proves the concept but does not serve this market.

A local business that combines cleaning, organization, and genuine understanding of elderly/disabled needs would own a space that no national chain is filling in Josephine County.
