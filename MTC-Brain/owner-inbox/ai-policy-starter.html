AI Policy Starter

A simple framework for small nonprofits and mission-driven businesses. Fill it out in 30 minutes. No technical background needed.

Why this matters. If your staff are using AI tools (and they probably are), you need a basic policy. Not because AI is dangerous. Because your organization's data, your clients' information, and your reputation deserve a plan. This one-pager gets you started.
1
What AI Tools Are We Using?
~10 min
Ask your team: what AI tools do you use for work? Include anything. ChatGPT, Grammarly, AI features in Canva, voice assistants, AI in your email. List them here.

What Should Never Go Into an AI Tool?
~5 min
Check every box that applies to your organization. These are types of information that should never be typed, pasted, or uploaded into any AI tool.
  • Client or beneficiary names, addresses, or contact information
  • Social Security numbers, tax IDs, or financial account numbers
  • Donor names, gift amounts, or fundraising data
  • Health information, case notes, or service records
  • Employee personnel files, salaries, or performance reviews
  • Passwords, login credentials, or API keys
  • Internal financial reports, bank statements, or budget drafts
  • Board meeting minutes or confidential governance documents
  • Any information covered by a confidentiality or grant agreement

3
Who Approves New AI Tools?
~5 min
Before anyone signs up for a new AI tool on behalf of the organization, who needs to say yes? Pick one or two people. Keep it simple.
AI tools must be approved by:
Before approving, that person will check:
  • Does this tool's privacy policy say they can train on our data?
  • Does it cost anything (now or after a trial)?
  • Does it connect to any of our existing accounts or databases?
  • Is there a free or paid tier, and which do we need?
  • Can we delete our data if we stop using it?
4
Our Acceptable Use Statement
~10 min
Read this statement together as a team. Fill in the blanks. Sign it. Post it where staff can see it. That is your policy for now.

At (organization name), we use AI tools to help us work more effectively. We do not use them as a replacement for human judgment, and we take responsibility for everything we publish, send, or submit, whether AI helped create it or not.

Before using any AI tool for work, staff will confirm that no restricted data (see Section 2) is being entered. Any AI-generated content that goes to clients, donors, board members, or the public must be reviewed by a human before it is sent.

New AI tools require approval from (see Section 3) before use. Staff are encouraged to explore AI tools and bring suggestions to the team. We are learning together.
