# Product Requirement Document: AI Policy Builder

## App Name
**PolicyReady**

## One-Line Summary
A guided, step-by-step tool that walks small nonprofits through building a complete, customized AI use policy. No templates. No guesswork.

## The Problem

92% of nonprofits now use AI in some form. But 47% have no AI policy at all. Only 4% have documented, repeatable AI workflows. And just 7% report major organizational improvements from their AI investments.

That is a staggering gap. Organizations adopted the tools before they built the rules. Staff are experimenting on their own. Nobody is tracking what is being used, how, or what data is being exposed.

60% of nonprofits lack in-house AI expertise. Only 4% have dedicated AI training budgets. So the policy never gets written. Not because people do not care, but because nobody on the team knows how to write one, and they cannot afford to hire someone who does.

Meanwhile, the policy conversations happening at the federal and state level are moving forward without nonprofit voices in the room. The sector's values (equity, community accountability, human judgment) are getting folded in after the fact, if at all.

Small orgs need a policy. They needed it a year ago. They just need a way to get it done without a consultant, a law firm, or a week of research.

## Who This Is For

- Executive directors of nonprofits with 3 to 50 staff
- Operations managers at mission-driven organizations who got handed "figure out our AI situation" with no budget
- Board members who know they need a policy but do not know what it should say
- Small business owners in rural or underserved areas who use AI tools daily but have nothing written down
- Anyone who has Googled "nonprofit AI policy template" and found either a 40-page enterprise document or a one-page checklist that helps nobody

## Core Features

1. **Guided questionnaire.** The user answers plain-language questions about their organization. What tools do you use? Who uses them? What kind of data do you handle? Do you work with minors, health data, or financial records? The tool adapts based on answers.

2. **Policy document generation.** Based on the answers, the tool generates a complete AI use policy in plain English. Not a template with blanks. A finished document with the org's name, tools, and rules baked in.

3. **Risk flagging.** If the user's answers reveal high-risk situations (like staff using ChatGPT with client data and no guidelines), the tool flags it clearly and explains why it matters.

4. **Section-by-section editing.** After the policy is generated, the user can review and edit each section individually. Change the language. Adjust the rules. Make it theirs.

5. **Export options.** Download the finished policy as a PDF or Word document. Ready to print, share with the board, or attach to an employee handbook.

6. **Version tracking.** Save and update the policy over time. AI tools change fast. The policy should be a living document, and the tool should make it easy to revisit and revise.

7. **Plain-language glossary.** Every section of the generated policy includes a short explanation of why it matters. Not legalese. Just context so the reader understands what they are agreeing to.

## What It Does NOT Do

- It does not provide legal advice. The output is a policy framework, not a legal document. Users should have their attorney review it if needed.
- It does not monitor compliance. It builds the policy. It does not watch whether people follow it.
- It does not replace training. A policy without training is just a file in a drawer. The tool should recommend training, but it does not deliver it.

## Why This Matters

Small and rural nonprofits are using AI every day. They are writing grant applications with it, summarizing meeting notes, managing donor communications. But they are doing it without guardrails. That puts their data, their clients, and their reputations at risk.

The organizations that serve the most vulnerable communities are the ones least likely to have policies in place. That is not a tech problem. It is a capacity problem. PolicyReady closes the gap between "we know we need this" and "we actually have it."

This is the exact space I work in. I help small orgs build the rules and systems they need to use technology responsibly. PolicyReady takes what I do in a consulting engagement and puts a version of it in the hands of orgs that cannot afford one-on-one work. It extends my reach without watering down the substance.

## Estimated Build Scope

**Medium.** The questionnaire logic and conditional branching are the most complex parts. The policy generation requires good writing and a well-structured content model, but it is not technically heavy. No integrations with external systems needed for v1. The main investment is in the content and logic, not the engineering.

## Revenue Model

- **Free tier.** Let users complete the questionnaire and see a summary of what their policy should cover. Enough to be useful. Not enough to replace the full tool.
- **Paid tier. $49 to $99 per policy generation.** One-time fee. The user gets the full, customized policy document with export and editing.
- **Annual update subscription. $29/year.** Access to revisit, update, and regenerate their policy as tools and best practices change.
- **Consulting upsell.** For orgs that want hands-on help implementing the policy, training their team, or customizing beyond what the tool covers, offer a direct path to book a consulting session. This is where the higher-margin work lives.
- **Bulk pricing for networks.** Offer a group rate to umbrella organizations, state associations, or funding intermediaries who want to provide the tool to their grantees or members. $30 to $50 per org at volume.
