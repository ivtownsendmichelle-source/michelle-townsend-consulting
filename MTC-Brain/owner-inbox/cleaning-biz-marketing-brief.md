## WHAT THIS IS

A full marketing firm engagement for a new house cleaning business specializing in elderly and disabled clients. Rural Southern Oregon. Focus markets: Cave Junction and Grants Pass.

Deliverable: 30 days of ready-to-post content across LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter/X, and Facebook. Every post includes caption, hashtags, and a photo brief.

---

## THE BUSINESS

- **What it does:** House cleaning with a specialization in organization for elderly and disabled people who need help maintaining their homes.
- **Who it serves:** Elderly and disabled adults. Possibly adult children managing care for aging parents.
- **Where:** Cave Junction, OR (rural, small community) and Grants Pass, OR (larger, more services available).
- **Differentiator:** Specialization. Not just cleaning. Organization included. Rural coverage. Possibly one of few or only services in Cave Jct area.
- **Brand voice to develop:** Trustworthy, warm, local. Speaks to families as much as to the person being served. Not clinical. Not corporate.

## MAXWELL — DELEGATION INSTRUCTIONS

Spin up the following agents simultaneously:

### 1. Researcher

Find the following and document with sources:

- Existing house cleaning services operating in Cave Junction and Grants Pass. Who they are, what they charge, what they do and don't offer.
- Any specific gap for elderly/disabled-focused cleaning in these markets.
- Demographics: population 65+ in Josephine County and surrounding area. Income levels. Percentage of rural elderly living alone.
- Medicaid/Medicare or ODHS programs that might fund or refer cleaning services in Oregon. Relevant because families often look for covered options.
- Facebook groups in Cave Jct and GP area where this kind of business would be seen by the right audience.
- Keywords and search terms people in this region actually use when looking for these services.
- What national competitors (like MaidPro or Molly Maid) do NOT do that a local specialized service could own.

Deliver: 1-2 page research brief. Plain language. No fluff. Sources cited.

### 2. Lead Strategist

Wait for Researcher output. Then build:

- Positioning statement. One sentence. What this business is, for who, and why it's different.
- Brand voice guide. 5-7 rules. How this business sounds in writing.
- Platform strategy. Which platform does what job for this business. Why LinkedIn is different from Facebook is different from Instagram for this audience.
- 30-day content arc. What story does the first month tell? How does it build trust week by week?
- Hook lines. 3-5 opening lines that could work across multiple posts.

Deliver: Strategy brief. 2-3 pages max.

### 3. Sally

Wait for both Researcher and Strategist output. Then write:

**30 posts per platform as follows:**

**LinkedIn (10 posts)**  
Audience: Adult children, care managers, referral partners, healthcare workers.  
Tone: Professional but warm. Trust-building. Local expertise.  
Format: Short personal story or insight. 150-250 words. 3-5 hashtags.  
Photo brief: What image would work with this post.

**Instagram (10 posts)**  
Audience: Local community. Families. People searching for help.  
Tone: Human. Before/after framing. Visual-first.  
Format: Caption 80-150 words. 8-12 hashtags. Strong photo brief required.  
Photo brief: Specific. Describe the shot. Light, angle, what's in frame.

**Twitter/X (10 posts)**  
Audience: Broader community discovery.  
Tone: Short, specific, local pride.  
Format: Under 240 characters. 2-3 hashtags only.  
Photo brief: Optional but preferred.

**Facebook (10 posts)**  
Audience: Cave Jct and GP community groups. Families. Word-of-mouth amplification.  
Tone: Neighbor voice. Warm. Practical.  
Format: 100-200 words. Local group sharing in mind. 3-5 hashtags.  
Photo brief: Relatable, real, not stock-looking.

Voice rules for all platforms:  
- No em dashes  
- No corporate language  
- Sounds like a real person from this community  
- No fake urgency  
- Read every line out loud. If it sounds like an ad, rewrite it.

Deliver: Complete 30-day calendar. One document, organized by day. Each post includes: day number, platform, caption, hashtags, photo brief.
