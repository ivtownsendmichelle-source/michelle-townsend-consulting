## 1. AI Governance and Data Policy

The gap is not AI adoption. 92% of nonprofits now use AI in some form. The gap is governance. 47% of nonprofits have no AI policy at all. Only 4% have documented, repeatable AI workflows. And just 7% report major organizational improvements from their AI investments.

The problem is structural: organizations adopted tools before building the rules, training, or oversight to use them well. 60% of nonprofits lack in-house AI expertise. Only 4% have dedicated AI training budgets. Staff are experimenting individually, not as teams, and nobody is tracking outcomes in any systematic way.

This is a direct match for Michelle's AI policy and data governance service line.

## 2. Cybersecurity and Data Protection

4 out of 5 nonprofits have no cybersecurity plan. 9 out of 10 do not train staff regularly on cybersecurity. 71% allow staff to use unsecured personal devices to access organizational email and files. 60% of nonprofits have experienced a cyberattack in the last two years, and breaches take an average of 467 days to discover.

The average cost of a data breach for a nonprofit is up to $2 million. For small and rural orgs, that is existential. Wipfli's 2026 survey found 60% of nonprofit leaders cite cost as their primary barrier to improving technology infrastructure, which means cybersecurity gets deprioritized even as the threat grows.

This connects directly to Michelle's organizational hygiene work. Password management, access controls, document systems. These are entry points for better security posture, and most small nonprofits cannot afford a dedicated security consultant.

## 3. Legacy System Modernization and Integration

Nearly half of nonprofits still use manual data entry and spreadsheets for compliance documentation, meeting summaries, case notes, and core operations. Donor databases, program tracking, and financial systems often exist as disconnected silos. Wipfli's survey confirms that technology maturity in nonprofits consistently lags behind other sectors.

The cost barrier is real. 60% cite budget as the top obstacle. But the deeper problem is capacity. Organizations do not have staff who can evaluate what to replace, plan a migration, or train a team on a new system. The result is that many nonprofits are stuck on outdated platforms that create security risks, waste staff time, and limit their ability to report outcomes to funders.

This connects to Michelle's custom tool builds and organizational hygiene services. Small orgs do not need enterprise solutions. They need someone who understands their workflows and can build or configure the right tools at the right scale.

## Flag: Something Michelle Should Know

Nonprofits are largely absent from public policy debates about AI regulation. Nonprofit Quarterly flagged this explicitly. The values that matter most to the sector (equity, community accountability, human judgment) are being incorporated into AI policy after decisions are already made, if at all. Michelle is already positioned in the AI policy space for small orgs. There may be an opportunity to also position as a voice in broader policy conversations, even informally, through writing or speaking. That is a differentiator almost nobody else in her market is claiming.

## What I Could Not Find

- **Sector-specific data for rural nonprofits.** Most reports survey nonprofits broadly. Data broken out specifically for rural or small-budget organizations is scarce. The Wipfli survey (n=299) and the NonProfit PRO AI adoption report (n=346) are the best available but do not segment by geography or org size in their public findings.
- **Hard numbers on how many nonprofits have experienced AI-related data incidents.** The governance gap is well documented, but actual incident data tied to AI misuse in nonprofits does not appear in any 2026 report I found. This is likely because it is too early and reporting mechanisms are not in place.
- **NTEN's own published research reports for 2026.** NTEN's publications page references past reports, and their 2026 conference agenda covers these topics, but I could not access a standalone 2026 research report from NTEN with downloadable findings. Their Nonprofit Tech Readiness cohort materials may contain data but are not publicly available.
